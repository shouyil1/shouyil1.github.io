About Me

I'm a graduate student at Columbia University majoring in Computer Science Machine Learning Track. I'm passionate about using AI and software/hardware solutions to tackle real-world challenges, particularly in areas like medical imaging and high-performance computing. My research and work experiences primarily focus on optimizing large-scale neural networks and deploying AI systems on edge devices to improve efficiency and scalability. Previously, I worked as an LLM Inference Architecture Engineer Intern at Meituan, where I focused on large-scale LLM optimization for deployment.

I am passionate about the intersection of AI, software engineering, and high-performance computing. During my time at the USC FPGA/Parallel Computing Lab, I focused on optimizing inference for Vision Transformers (ViTs) and accelerating computations on FPGA platforms under the mentorship of Professor Viktor Prasanna.

During my time at the USC Convergent Science Institute in Cancer, I worked on Deep Morphological Profiling of Immune Cells in Peripheral Blood under the mentorship of Dr. Peter Kuhn and Amin Naghdloo. This work ignited my passion in understanding immune responses in cancer research.

When not working, I enjoy going to the gym, watching sports, and exploring delicious local cuisines with my friends.
